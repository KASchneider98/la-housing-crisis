Project Overview

We are currently in a housing affordability crisis with the cost of living rapidly increasing. This makes it harder for people to purchase homes and afford daily neccesities. Los Angeles County is expereincing a major housing affordability crisis. This crisis is taking place all over the state of California, however this project focuses on 10 major cities in Los Angeles County. Between 2020 and 2024, there has been a 15-20% increase of median home prices however incomes only grew to 7-10%. This creates a larger affordability gap because it makes homeownership out of reach for many of the residents who live there. The maps below examine the affordability crisis across 10 cities in LA county that compare median home values and household incomes from the years 2020 and 2024. This shows how rapidly housing costs and rising costs compare to the growth of income, worsening the financial strain that affect families and communities throughout Los Angeles County.

LA Housing 2020 vs. 2024

This map shows 2020 and 2024 Housing Costs as well as median household income and median mortgage costs. Darker circles indicate high prices as well as the difference between 2020 and 2024. The diamond symbol shows the median mortgage cost between 2020 and 2024.

Income Changes

This map shows the change in median household incomes from 2020 to 2024. Click on a city to see the change in income from 2020 t0 2024. Higher incomes are in red, lower are in orange and yellow.

Important Info:

  • Los Angeles: Home prices rose from $697K to $815K: 17% increase
  • Beverly Hills: Prices jumped from $1.58M to $1.82M: 15% increase
  • Compton: Even affordable areas saw increases from $418K to $485K: 16% increase
  • Income Growth: Income from 2020 to 2024 only increased 7-10%
  • Affordability Gap: Housing costs grew 2-3x faster than incomes
